Human Rights

Article 26: Right to Education

Everyone has the right to education. Education shall be free, at least in the elementary and fundamental stages. Elementary education shall be compulsory. Technical and professional education shall be made generally available and higher education shall be equally accessible to all on the basis of merit.

Article 29: Aims of Education

Education shall be directed to the full development of the human personality and to the strengthening of respect for human rights and fundamental freedoms. It shall promote understanding, tolerance, and friendship among all nations, racial or religious groups, and shall further the activities of the United Nations for the maintenance of peace.

Current Circumstances

NEET UG 2024 Scam in India

The NEET UG 2024 exam has faced significant controversy due to allegations of irregularities, paper leaks, and result manipulations. Following the exam on May 5, 2024, and the results announced on June 4, students raised concerns about inflated cutoffs and exceptionally high marks, with many candidates achieving top ranks. Specific issues included discrepancies in textbooks and compensatory marks given to some students, leading to widespread suspicion and demands for a re-examination.

Cheating Scandal in South Korea's Suneung Exam

South Korea's Suneung, the highly competitive college entrance exam, was recently hit by a scandal involving students using concealed devices to receive answers during the test. This incident has sparked a national debate on the integrity of the examination system and the measures needed to prevent future occurrences.

Fake Grant Application Scam in South Africa

A fraudulent scheme emerged in 2024 where scammers circulated fake grant application forms, claiming to be from the South African Social Security Agency (SASSA). This scam targeted vulnerable citizens, urging them to apply through unofficial links, risking identity theft.
